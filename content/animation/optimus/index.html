This is the final product. A total of 77 shots were used to compose this animation.

Having fun in the studio

After getting the shot framed up with the green screen in the back and the lights setup, it was time to start the arduous process of moving the subject 1 frame at a time.

Here you can see nearly all of the setup. One additional softbox was placed on the left side of the scene to give fill light. The blue panel is to offset any green bounce and the yellow hue from the lights, bringing a more balanced color to the subject.

Those lights get hot! Each move was done with care to make sure the feet did not move out of place from the last movement...otherwise the animated composite would look like it was "sliding" all around.

A still of Optimus opening his chest compartment. I did the animation on glass so that there would be less rotoscoping around the feet once I took the images into post. Next time I would have cleaned the glass off better, but it ended up working pretty well.

After I had captured the entire stop motion performance, I had to find a suitable location to superimpose my mechanical hero onto. Since it was summer and HOT outside, I decided to try a cool indoor spot that had a nice warm tone to it. This is the reference plate used for the background of the shot.

To make sure things would look right, I brought Optimus along with me to set him in the scene for scale and lighting purposes. I had very specfic requirements on lighting since the studio had been setup a certain way, so I had to make sure the sunlight matched as closely as possible. I had also taken measurements from distance of the camera sensor to the subject and recreated those conditions in this test to make sure everything lined up and looked right.

Optimus Prime Comes to Life

I had always appreciated the work of Hollywoods greatest stop motion special effects wizard Ray Harryhausen. As a kid I would construct simple stop motion animations first with my parents Polaroid camera, and then disposable point and shoots when they were available. After years absence I decided it would be a fun personal project to take two things I loved as a kid, Stop Motion Animation and Transformers, and do one of my own.
